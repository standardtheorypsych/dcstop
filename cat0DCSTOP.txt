Diagnostic Criteria for The Standard Theory of Psychology

Severity: Cyclic Thinking and the Affliction of Memory
Normal: Circular, Ovular, Elliptical, Incongruent
Abnormal: Disruption, Derangement, Illness, Disease

Physical Irregular Impulse: Components of Impulse
Normal: Impulse, Subtraction Compression
Abnormal: any alternate process

Intimacy Dysfunction: Problems communicating directly, one-on-one
Normal: Constructive Intimacy
Abnormal: Any alternate process in self/one-on-one communication, especially body status

Social Identity Disorder: Replacing one-on-one communication with environment, objects, relationships, social status, or ideas
Normal: Social (idea, item, interpretation), Shared (subversion, aversion), Supersocial 
          (grouping and sorting)
Abnormal:
          Social: Social Identity Disorder Addiction, Social Intimacy
          Shared: Aversive Identity Disorder (Forced Identity Disorder), Subversive Identity 
                Disorder
          Supersocial: Personification/Depersonification, Conditional Relationship/Disinterest,
                Pseudo-directive/Pseudo-deferral, Gapping

Behavioral Irregular Impulse: Externalized behavior caused by hormones, medication, environment, internal stimulation, relationships, substances, unresolved communication, etc.
